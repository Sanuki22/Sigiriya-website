Our Mission 
To preserve and promote the cultural and historical significance of Sigiriya, while also creating sustainable tourism opportunities that benefit local communities and the environment. This mission could involve developing educational programs for visitors, supporting local businesses and artisans, implementing eco-friendly practices, and working with government agencies to protect the site from damage or exploitation. Additionally, efforts could be made to engage with local communities and stakeholders to ensure that their perspectives and needs are taken into account in any decisions related to Sigiriya's management and development. Ultimately, the goal would be to create a model for responsible tourism that respects and celebrates the unique heritage of Sigiriya, while also promoting economic growth and environmental stewardship.
Latest Updates 
Sigiriya will be declared the first eco-friendly tourist destination in the country free of plastic and polythene this year Tourism Minister Prasanna Ranatunga stated.
 
He said the Ministry of Tourism and the Tourism Development Authority are working in collaboration with institutions such as the Central Cultural Fund and the Wildlife Department in the effort.
 
Minister Ranatunga who visited the Sigiriya Tourist Zone Wednesday (16) further stated that the government is also taking steps to expand the facilities required by local and foreign tourists visiting Sigiriya.
 
There are plans to expand drinking water, sanitation and parking facilities without any harm to the the Sigiriya ecosystem. The Minister also said that discussions are underway with various parties to obtain the relevant provisions.
 
The Minister said that the government has also taken steps to establish domestic air facilities to Sigiriya for the convenience of local and foreign tourists and that steps have already been taken to expedite the resolution of the problems faced by those engaged in the tourism industry in the Sigiriya tourist area. .
